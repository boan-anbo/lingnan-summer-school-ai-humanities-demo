\section*{Where Did Lingnan Begin?}

The strongest source currently in hand is a 1908 institutional publication,
\emph{Canton Christian College: its field and work in China}. It describes the
college as opening in Canton, and then says that in June 1900 ``the school was
moved to Macao,'' where it remained until it was removed to Canton in 1904.\footnote{
Canton Christian College, \emph{Canton Christian College: its field and work in
China} (1908), p. 12.}

A later retrospective text, \emph{Fifty Years Ago at Lingnan}, appears to
remember a founding in Macau around 1899. That source is useful, but later. A
cautious conclusion is therefore not that Lingnan was simply founded in Macau,
but that Macau was an important temporary site in the early history of a
Canton/Guangzhou institution.
