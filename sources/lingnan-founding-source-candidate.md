# Source Candidate Record

- **Query/path:** Was Lingnan University founded in Guangzhou or Macau?
- **Best source (PDF):** *Canton Christian College: its field and work in China* (1908).
- **Owner/collection:** Digital Commons @ Lingnan University, Records of Lingnan University (Guangzhou, 1888-1952).
- **URL/citation:** https://commons.ln.edu.hk/lingnan_trustees_records/2/
- **Local path:** `materials/canton-christian-college-field-work-china-1908.pdf`
- **Date/author:** 1908, institutional publication by Canton Christian College.
- **Source type:** Open-access institutional repository PDF / scan.
- **Why best:** It is a downloadable file with clear repository provenance, date, collection, citation, and a page image that can be inspected directly. It is closer to the events than later retrospective accounts.
- **Relevant wording to inspect:** Page 12 discusses the school being moved to Macao in 1900 and later removed to Canton in 1904.
- **Lead suggests (not a final answer):** Macao was an important temporary site in the early institutional history. This does not by itself prove that Lingnan was simply founded in Macao.
- **Problems:** It is still an institutional publication, not a complete archive of trustee minutes or correspondence. The exact founding claim needs comparison with earlier records and later retrospective accounts.
- **Next check:** Compare with `Fifty Years Ago at Lingnan` and with earlier trustee or mission records if available.
